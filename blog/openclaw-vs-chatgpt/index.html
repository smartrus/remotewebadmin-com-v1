OpenClaw vs ChatGPT: Which Personal AI Assistant Is Right for You?

The personal AI assistant market has two broad categories: hosted platforms you sign up for, and self-hosted open-source gateways you control. ChatGPT is the most recognisable name in the first category. OpenClaw is rapidly becoming the leading option in the second. This comparison breaks down the key differences to help you decide which is right for your situation.

Privacy: Where OpenClaw Has a Clear Advantage

When you use ChatGPT, your conversations are processed on OpenAI’s servers. OpenAI’s data usage policy allows them to use your conversations to improve their models (unless you opt out via account settings, which many users never find or do). Even with opt-out, your data still transits OpenAI’s infrastructure.

With OpenClaw self-hosted deployment, your messages go: your phone → your server → the AI provider API → back to your server → back to your phone. The AI provider (OpenAI, Anthropic, etc.) still sees the content of your AI queries - but your conversation history, contact identities, and messaging metadata stay on your server. No third-party company is storing a record of every conversation you’ve had.

For business users with confidential information, or individuals simply uncomfortable with their thoughts and questions being logged by a large tech company, this distinction matters.

Cost: OpenClaw Is Significantly Cheaper

A ChatGPT Plus subscription costs $20/month for one user. ChatGPT Pro is $200/month.

A self-hosted OpenClaw instance costs:

  • VPS: $5–6/month (DigitalOcean or Hetzner basic tier)
  • AI API costs: typically $1–5/month for personal use (pay-per-token)
  • Total: $6–11/month

Over one year: ChatGPT Plus costs $240. OpenClaw costs $72–132 - a saving of $108–168 per year. For teams, the gap widens significantly: five ChatGPT Plus seats cost $100/month ($1,200/year), while OpenClaw Business on a slightly larger VPS can serve the same team for $15–25/month total.

The one caveat: if your API usage is high (many long conversations per day), token costs can increase. For typical personal use, $1–5/month in API costs is accurate.

Customisation: No Contest

ChatGPT allows “custom instructions” - a system-level note you can set telling the AI your name, role, and preferences. That’s essentially the extent of personalisation for standard users.

OpenClaw customisation is a different class entirely:

  • Full system prompts per channel, agent, or user
  • Plugin architecture for external tools (web search, calendar, memory, custom APIs)
  • Multiple AI models simultaneously - use Claude 3.5 Sonnet for writing tasks, GPT-4o for coding
  • Custom personas per channel (one agent for work Discord, another for personal Telegram)
  • Persistent memory that accumulates context over time

Messaging Channels: OpenClaw Wins

ChatGPT is available via web browser and the ChatGPT iOS/Android app. That’s it. You access it on ChatGPT’s terms - their interface, their app.

OpenClaw connects to the apps you already use: WhatsApp, Telegram, Discord, and iMessage. There’s no new app to remember, no separate login to juggle. Your AI is embedded in your existing communication workflow - the same place you talk to family, colleagues, and friends.

OpenClaw wins on privacy, cost, customisation, and channel breadth. ChatGPT wins on setup simplicity - which is a solved problem when you have someone to do the setup for you.
